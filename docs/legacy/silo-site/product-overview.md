# Product Overview

Language: [pt-BR](pt-br/product-overview.md) | `English`

## Problem

Brazilian lawyers work across disconnected silos:

- their own document corpus
- case law spread across multiple sources
- legislation consulted outside the case context
- general-purpose LLMs with no structural memory or traceability

The result is often a fragmented, time-consuming, and weakly auditable
workflow.

## What Silo does

Silo brings these layers together into a single legal intelligence system.

Instead of treating research, grounding, and document context as separate
tasks, it tries to connect everything that matters to the reading of a case:

- what already exists in the lawyer's own corpus
- how courts decided similar problems
- which legal provisions actually apply
- where gaps, ambiguities, and risks still exist

## Principles

### 1. Better than an LLM-only workflow, not against LLMs

Silo starts from the assumption that the real competitor is the workflow
built around a general-purpose LLM. The goal is not generic chat, but
grounding, structure, and traceability that the model alone does not have.

### 2. Traceability matters

A good legal answer must allow inspection. The system should show where the
data came from, what is retrieved evidence, and what is derived synthesis.

### 3. One product, specialized gears

For users, buyers, and observers, there is one product: `Silo`.
Internally, the work is organized into specialized modules.

### 4. Utility before feature volume

The focus is to solve real legal workflow pain points, not to pile up AI
features with low adoption.

## Maturity status

Silo is currently in a private build and validation phase.

Current priorities include:

- data and infrastructure foundations
- proving value above an LLM-only workflow
- external validation through real cases

## What it is not

Silo is not:

- an open legal repository
- a generic chatbot
- a replacement for human legal work
- a simple wrapper around an LLM API
