Data Room / 02 · System

The system in five minutes

Silo materializes the legal syllogism into a navigable knowledge graph. This page walks the architecture from raw decisions to a queryable structure that LLMs and lawyers can both reason against.

Why a graph, not a vector store

Vector retrieval finds passages that look similar to a query. It cannot answer the questions a litigator actually asks: which precedents bind this court, which decisions overrule which, which arguments tend to win when the panel includes these justices. The unit of legal reasoning is the relationship between decisions, not the text of a single one.

The hard problem

Cross-tribunal entity resolution. The same justice writes under five name variants. The same statute is cited eight ways. The same precedent appears as a citation in court A and as a holding in court B. Resolving these into stable identities is what makes the graph useful — and is the part competitors who index PDFs cannot do.
