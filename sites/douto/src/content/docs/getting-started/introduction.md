# Introduction

Douto is the doctrine pipeline of the sens.legal ecosystem. It processes legal books into structured doctrinal artifacts that can strengthen the ecosystem's backend knowledge layer.

## The problem Douto solves

Doctrinal research is hard to operationalize. Legal books contain valuable concepts, positions, and framing, but they usually live in PDFs, scattered notes, or manual reading workflows that are difficult to reuse downstream.

Douto exists to turn that material into:

- structured chunks
- searchable doctrinal metadata
- embeddings and artifacts that can be consumed later

## What Douto is

Douto is:

- a batch-processing pipeline
- a structured doctrine knowledge base
- a supplier of doctrinal artifacts into the broader ecosystem

It is not:

- the frontend hub for lawyers
- the owner of jurisprudence retrieval
- the legislation authority
- a finished standalone doctrine product for end users

## Where Douto fits

Within sens.legal:

| Project | Responsibility |
|---------|----------------|
| **Juca** | Frontend hub |
| **Valter** | Central jurisprudence and reasoning backend |
| **Leci** | Legislation grounding |
| **Douto** | Doctrine artifact production |

The key relationship is between Douto and Valter. Douto produces doctrinal material that can feed Valter's broader knowledge layer. That is why Douto should be described as an internal supplier, not an autonomous user-facing agent.

## Core concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Chunk** | A doctrinal fragment produced from legal books |
| **Enrichment** | Metadata classification over doctrinal content |
| **Embedding** | Vector representation used in doctrinal retrieval workflows |
| **Artifact** | A reusable output generated by the pipeline for downstream consumption |

## Practical takeaway

If you are reading these docs from the perspective of the full ecosystem, think of Douto as the doctrine production layer that supports Valter, not as a finished standalone product for lawyers.
